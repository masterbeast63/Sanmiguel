THE MAP OF BOHOL

Behold BOHOL

Bohol is an island province located in the Central Visayas region of the Philippines. It is known for its beautiful beaches, diverse wildlife, and unique geological formations. One of the most famous attractions in Bohol is the Chocolate Hills, a series of over 1,000 grass-covered limestone hills that turn brown during the dry season, resembling chocolate mounds. Another popular destination is the Tarsier Sanctuary, where visitors can see the world's smallest primate, the tarsier, in their natural habitat. Bohol is also home to several stunning beaches, such as Alona Beach and Dumaluan Beach, which offer clear blue waters and white sand. Visitors can enjoy activities such as swimming, snorkeling, and scuba diving in these beaches. The culture of Bohol is deeply rooted in its history, and visitors can see this through its old churches, such as the Baclayon Church and the Loboc Church, both of which were built during the Spanish colonial period. Overall, Bohol is a beautiful and unique destination that offers a mix of natural wonders, cultural heritage, and outdoor activities.
